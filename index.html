About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Statistics at Rutgers University, specializing in statistical machine learning, high-dimensional inference, and deep generative models. I have 3+ years of quantitative finance experience at Rabobank (New York), building forecasting and risk tools for derivatives portfolios.

My current interests include dynamic factor models and sparse state-space inference; VAEs, flows, and diffusion models; causal inference in finance and healthcare; and machine-learning–based stochastic weather generators for renewable energy.

Research

Dynamic Factor Models & Sparse State-Space Inference

Identifiable, high-dimensional DFMs with episodic factor activation and efficient MAP-EM/Kalman filtering.

Deep Generative Models

VAEs, normalizing flows, and diffusion models for complex temporal structure and stochastic volatility.

Causal ML in Finance & Health

Combining causal inference with deep learning for order book dynamics and clinical EEG analyses.

Stochastic Weather Generators

Minute-level wind vector generation trained on multi-decade atmospheric datasets for grid planning.

Work in Progress

  • Dynamic Vector Factor Model with Sparse Factor Transitions

    Novel Linear-Gaussian DFM with time-varying spike-and-slab innovations; MAP-EM with Kalman filter/RTS smoother. Episodic factor activation improves identifiability and forecasts.

  • ML Stochastic Weather Generator (with Michael Stein)

    Minute-scale wind vector generator trained on ~30 years of ARM Lamont data using VAEs and flows; complements physics-based models for renewable grid planning.

  • Causal Inference in High-Frequency Markets (with Regina Liu)

    Transformer-based approach for causal discovery in microsecond order-book dynamics; addresses confounding while remaining computationally efficient.
